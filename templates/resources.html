Quick Start Guide

Wingspan trades 0DTE (zero days to expiration) SPX butterfly spreads. You buy two butterflies at the open — one call-side, one put-side — centered around the opening price. During the day, you either trap the winning side or hedge against extreme moves. Everything expires at 4:00 PM ET.

1

Pre-Market (9:00–9:30 AM)

Open Wingspan and click Analyze & Generate Strategy. The engine fetches live SPX/VIX data and calculates your strikes, widths, and trap alerts. Copy the option chains to your broker.

2

Open (9:30–9:35 AM)

Buy both butterfly spreads as limit orders near the mid price. The call fly and put fly should fill at roughly equal debits. Your total risk is the combined debit paid.

3

Monitor (9:35 AM–1:30 PM)

Watch for SPX to breach your trap alert levels (shown on the strategy page). No action needed until then — the position profits from theta decay as the day progresses.

4

Trap or Hedge (1:30–2:30 PM)

If trap score is 🟢 GREEN/YELLOW: SPX has moved into one fly's profit zone. Buy the center trap butterfly to lock in gains. Close the losing side for partial credit.

If trap score is 🔴 RED: Extended move day — skip the trap. Buy a directional spread in the move direction to convert the loss into a win.

5

Expiration (3:30–4:00 PM)

SPX options settle on the closing print at 4:00 PM ET. If trapped, hold to expiry. If not trapped, close both sides in the last 30 minutes to recover residual value.

Requirements

Broker Requirements

  • SPX options access — must be able to trade S&P 500 index options (not SPY)
  • Multi-leg orders — ability to enter butterfly spreads as a single order
  • 0DTE trading enabled — some brokers restrict same-day expiration trades
  • Level 3 options approval — required for spread trading at most brokers

Compatible brokers: TD Ameritrade/Schwab, Interactive Brokers, Tastytrade, E*TRADE. Check with your broker for specific 0DTE policies.

Capital Requirements

  • Minimum per contract: ~$800–$2,000 depending on VIX (covers the opening debit for both flies)
  • Recommended starting capital: $5,000–$10,000 for 1–3 contracts with proper position sizing
  • Pattern Day Trader rule: Not applicable — SPX options are cash-settled index options, not equities
  • Margin: Butterfly spreads are defined-risk. Maximum loss = debit paid. No margin calls.

Knowledge Requirements

  • Understanding of butterfly spread mechanics (max profit at center, zero outside wings)
  • Comfort with options Greeks — particularly theta (time decay) and delta (directional exposure)
  • Ability to execute multi-leg orders quickly during market hours
  • Familiarity with SPX settlement procedures (PM settlement, cash-settled)

Time Commitment

  • Pre-market: 5 min — generate strategy, copy chains
  • Open: 5 min — enter orders
  • Midday: Passive monitoring — set price alerts at trap levels
  • Trap window: 10 min — execute trap or hedge at 1:30–2:30 PM
  • Total: ~20–30 minutes of active time per day

Daily Playbook

Position Structure

The "Wingspan" (a.k.a. "Batman") position is two butterfly spreads centered symmetrically around the SPX opening price:

  • Call butterfly: Open / Open+W / Open+2W — profits if SPX rises moderately
  • Put butterfly: Open / Open−W / Open−2W — profits if SPX falls moderately

With gap = 0 (recommended), the inner wings overlap at the open price, eliminating any dead zone. The combined position profits if SPX settles anywhere within ±2W of the open.

Width Sizing

Width is set to 60% of the VIX-implied expected move. This places the max profit peak at the most likely settlement point while keeping the outer wings beyond 1σ. The width scales automatically with SPX level and VIX — wider when volatility is high, narrower when it's low.

Trap Mechanics

When SPX moves directionally, one butterfly enters its profit zone. The trap locks in that profit by buying a third butterfly (1.5× wider) centered between the open and the winning fly's center. This widens the profit tent and reduces sensitivity to where SPX settles.

  • Trigger: SPX moves ≥15% of expected move from open (regime-adjusted)
  • Timing: Best at 1:30–2:30 PM when 70%+ of theta has decayed — trap debits are cheapest
  • Losing side: Close for ~15% of original debit to recover capital

Directional Hedge

On extended-move days (trap score RED, SPX beyond 1× EM), skip the trap. Instead, buy a cheap directional put or call spread in the direction of the move. Cost is ~$0.50 after midday. If SPX continues (77% of the time), the spread converts what would have been a full loss into a partial or full win.

Trap Score System

The composite trap score (0–100) grades each day's trap opportunity using three signals:

Move / EM (60% weight)

How far SPX has moved relative to the expected move. Below 0.7× EM = excellent (butterflies still in profit zone). Above 1.0× EM = danger (extended past the wings).

Range Expansion (30% weight)

Today's range vs the 5-day average. Contracted range = mean-reverting day = great for butterflies. Expanded range = trending day = skip the trap.

VIX Trend (10% weight)

VIX dropping from its 5-day average = stabilizing conditions = slightly better trap odds. Minor tiebreaker signal.

Hedging Extended Moves

~24% of days, SPX moves beyond 1× EM and both flies expire worthless. The directional hedge converts these losses:

1

Detect

By 12:30–1:00 PM, SPX has already moved past 1× EM. The trap score reads 🔴 RED (below 40). This signals an extended-move day.

2

Hedge

Buy a directional debit spread (call spread if SPX is up, put spread if down) at the current price. Width = 0.5× butterfly width. Cost: ~$0.30–$0.80 (cheap because 50%+ theta is already burned).

3

Hold

If SPX continues in the same direction (77% probability based on intraday stick rates), the spread pays out. The payoff often exceeds the combined batman debit + hedge cost, converting a loss into a net win.

💡 The hedge costs ~$50/contract, fires only on worst days, and in backtesting reduced max drawdown 57% while adding 12% to total return.

FAQ

Why SPX and not SPY?

SPX options are cash-settled (no stock assignment risk), European-style (no early exercise), and receive favorable 60/40 tax treatment under Section 1256. SPX also has tighter bid-ask spreads at standard strikes.

What's the worst-case loss per day?

Your maximum loss is the total debit paid for both butterflies plus any trap or hedge cost. Typically $8–$19 per point of width, or $800–$1,900 per contract. The directional hedge limits this further on extreme days.

Can I trade this with a small account?

Yes. Butterfly spreads are defined-risk. A single contract requires $800–$2,000 in buying power depending on the day's width and VIX. No margin expansion is possible since max loss = debit paid.

Do I need to watch the market all day?

No. Set price alerts at the trap trigger levels shown on the strategy page. The only active decision window is 1:30–2:30 PM (trap or hedge). Total active time is ~20 minutes per day.

What about commissions?

Each butterfly is 3 legs (buy/sell/buy). Most brokers charge $0.50–$0.65 per contract per leg. For a single batman position (2 flies = 6 legs), expect $3–$4 in commissions. Add another $3–$4 for the trap. Total: ~$6–$8 per day per contract.

How were the backtest results calculated?

All butterfly debits are priced using Black-Scholes with a 0.85× VIX discount (variance risk premium adjustment) and time-decay function. Real SPX/VIX OHLC data from Yahoo Finance, 2020–2026. See the Methodology page for complete details.

What happens on high-VIX days (VIX > 30)?

The engine automatically widens the butterflies (up to 80pt cap) to match the larger expected moves. The trap trigger also widens (25% of EM vs 15% in normal vol). High-vol days have higher debits but also higher max profit potential.
