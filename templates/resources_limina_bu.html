Investment Fundamentals

Compounding & Long‑Term Growth

Compounding is growth on growth—reinvested returns generate additional returns over time.

  • Start early—time in the market matters more than timing the market.
  • Keep costs low—expense ratios and fees compound against you.
  • Stay consistent—automatic contributions help you capture more compounding periods.

Learn more: Investopedia: Compounding · Khan Academy: Compound Interest

Dollar‑Cost Averaging (DCA)

DCA means investing a fixed amount on a regular schedule to reduce the impact of volatility.

  • Smooths entry points across market cycles.
  • Helps avoid large, poorly timed lump‑sum purchases.
  • Pairs well with automatic contributions (payroll or monthly transfers).

Learn more: Investopedia: DCA · Morningstar: DCA Overview

Diversification & Asset Allocation

Diversification spreads risk across assets; asset allocation aligns portfolios with risk tolerance and goals.

  • Mix asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash; optionally real assets) to reduce single‑asset risk.
  • Set a strategic allocation and rebalance periodically to maintain risk targets.
  • Use broad, low‑cost index ETFs for core exposures (e.g., total market, S&P 500, aggregate bonds).

Learn more: Investopedia: Diversification · Morningstar: Asset Allocation

Volatility, Drawdowns & Risk Management

Markets fluctuate. Risk management focuses on limiting large losses and staying invested appropriately.

  • Expect drawdowns—even long bull markets experience 10–20% declines.
  • Align allocation with your time horizon and capacity for loss.
  • Use rules‑based approaches (e.g., trend filters) to reduce prolonged bear‑market exposure.

Learn more: Investopedia: Drawdown · Morningstar: Risk

Taxes: STCG vs LTCG

Holding periods matter: short‑term capital gains (STCG) are typically taxed as ordinary income; long‑term (LTCG) often at lower rates.

  • Consider placing higher‑turnover strategies in tax‑advantaged accounts when possible.
  • Harvest losses thoughtfully and avoid wash‑sale violations.
  • Track cost basis and holding periods for accurate reporting.

Learn more: IRS Topic 409 – Capital Gains & Losses · Investopedia: Capital Gains Tax Guide

Account Types & Taxes (US)

Taxable Accounts

Standard brokerage accounts with capital gains/losses. Consider tax‑efficient holding periods and realized gains.

Tax‑Advantaged Accounts

  • Roth IRA – tax‑free growth/withdrawals (subject to rules)
  • Traditional IRA – tax‑deferred; withdrawals taxed as income
  • 401(k)/Solo‑401(k) – employer or self‑employed plans

Consult a qualified tax professional for your specific situation.
