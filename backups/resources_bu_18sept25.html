Signal Hawk Strategy – Overview

Signal Hawk is a long‑term, rules‑based alert service that seeks to outperform the S&P 500 by being invested during broad uptrends and reducing exposure during major drawdowns. Execution is typically through SPXL (3x S&P 500 ETF) for risk‑tolerant users or SPY/VOO for lower volatility.

  • Data-driven entries/exits using price‑trend regime filters
  • Downside‑mitigation rules to avoid prolonged bear markets
  • Focus on time in market during favorable regimes

Account Types & Taxes (US)

Taxable Accounts

Standard brokerage accounts with capital gains/losses. Consider tax‑efficient holding periods and realized gains.

Tax‑Advantaged Accounts

  • Roth IRA – tax‑free growth/withdrawals (subject to rules)
  • Traditional IRA – tax‑deferred; withdrawals taxed as income
  • 401(k)/Solo‑401(k) – employer or self‑employed plans

Consult a qualified tax professional for your specific situation.

Partnering with Brokerages (Monetization)

Traditional firms (Fidelity, Schwab, Vanguard) rarely offer public affiliate programs. Fintech platforms often do, via networks:

  • Apply as a publisher on affiliate networks (Impact, CJ, Rakuten)
  • Search for brokerage/referral campaigns (Robinhood, Webull, Public, SoFi, etc.)
  • Reach out directly to brokerage BD/affiliate teams with projected volumes
  • Maintain FTC‑compliant disclosures on your site

Typical economics: flat CPA per funded account; structure varies by platform and campaign.
