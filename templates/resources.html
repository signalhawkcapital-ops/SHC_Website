Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)

DCA means investing a fixed amount on a regular schedule to reduce the impact of volatility.

  • Smooths entry points across market cycles.
  • Helps avoid large, poorly timed lump-sum purchases.
  • Pairs well with automatic contributions (payroll or monthly transfers).
Learn more: Investopedia: DCA

Diversification & Asset Allocation

Diversification spreads risk across assets; asset allocation aligns portfolios with risk tolerance and goals.

  • Mix asset classes (stocks, bonds, cash; optionally real assets) to reduce single-asset risk.
  • Set a strategic allocation and rebalance periodically to maintain risk targets.
  • Use broad, low-cost index ETFs for core exposures (e.g., total market, S&P 500, aggregate bonds).
Learn more: Investopedia: Diversification

Volatility, Drawdowns & Risk Management

Markets fluctuate. Risk management focuses on limiting large losses and staying invested appropriately.

  • Expect drawdowns â€” even long bull markets experience 10â€“20% declines.
  • Align allocation with your time horizon and capacity for loss.

Taxes: STCG vs LTCG

Holding periods matter: short-term capital gains (STCG) are typically taxed as ordinary income; long-term (LTCG) often at lower rates.

  • Consider placing higher-turnover strategies in tax-advantaged accounts when possible.
  • Harvest losses thoughtfully and avoid wash-sale violations.
  • Track cost basis and holding periods for accurate reporting.
